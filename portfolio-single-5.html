Hue Knew 2024

Hue Knew was our way of breaking out of the usual data-viz formatâ€”while others built maps and infographics, we wanted to make a full game. Pulling open-source film data for the quizzes was the easy part; the real challenge was designing the Wordle-style modes and stats options that make the game feel alive. Its significance lies in choosing the harder path and learning the system by building it ourselves.

Hue Knew is an interactive web-based quiz that challenges users to identify cinematic genres based solely on curated color palettes. By reducing films to their chromatic signatures, the project foregrounds how color theory, visual perception, and genre conventions shape the emotional language of cinema. The interface design draws inspiration from Wordle, a game I play every day, adapting its grid-based structure, guess-and-feedback rhythm, and satisfying incremental reveal to create an intuitive, playful system for visual reasoning.

Curated palettes from a broad range of films are distilled into simplified swatches, which the interface presents as selectable challenges. Using a lightweight front-end framework, the platform manages user input, scoring logic, animated feedback, and branching state transitions. Color combinations are mapped to genre categories, producing immediate responses that reveal how strongly palette alone can signal narrative intent.

The project reframes film interpretation through chromatic structure rather than plot or character. By gamifying perceptual cues, Hue Knew? demonstrates how color functions as a storytelling shorthand while prompting players to reflect on their own cinematic assumptions and pattern-recognition habits.

A fully functional web-based quiz; curated film-palette dataset; Wordle-inspired interface system; project deployed online and tested with users.
