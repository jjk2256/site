Pre_CAPSTONE 2024

This project taught me that a strong narrative can snap every technical piece into place. After reading about Philippe Petit days before my final crit, the entire storyline finally clickedâ€”and so did the project. Pre_CAPSTONE establishes the conceptual starting point for the capstone work, rooted in the metaphor of urban foraging. The project explores how design can emerge from existing materials and overlooked ecologies, emphasizing immediacy and resourcefulness over invention. Upcycled plastics, reclaimed wood, early mycelium studies, and publicly available upcycling videos found online formed the basis for a material practice grounded in availability, shared knowledge, and context.

Material foraging was paired with digital foraging. Mapbox geodata contextualized locations, while Grasshopper environmental analysesâ€”solar exposure, shade, windâ€”guided spatial decisions. These studies were modeled in Rhino and consolidated into a GitHub-hosted interface for review and iteration.

The project treats both urban datasets and salvaged materials as found conditions rather than raw inputs to be replaced. This combination of ecological awareness and computational mapping forms a resource-efficient design methodology.

Conceptual framework defined; material tests completed; geospatial studies published as foundational documentation for the later capstone.

Developed through a combination of Mapbox and QGIS mapping workflows, Grasshopper optimization tools, and interactive web prototyping with D3.js.
