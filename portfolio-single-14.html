CAPSTONE_grow 2024

Biological Cultivation + Environmental Monitoring

Mycelium experiment failed miserably last semester, so finding the design center's support was a breakthrough. The director's guidance gave me the practice and conditions I'd been missing. That small victory is what made the monitored growth phase succeed.

Abstract
The Grow phase examines how biologically active mycelium colonizes a digitally fabricated scaffold under controlled environmental conditions. This stage evaluates the relationship between geometric formwork, substrate composition, and fungal behavior—using continuous sensing and time-lapse imaging to document colonization patterns and environmental responsiveness.

Background
Following robotic fabrication of the structural scaffold, the Grow phase shifts the investigation into biological formation. This segment explores how environmental parameters—temperature, humidity, and CO₂ concentration—shape mycelial development and how monitoring infrastructures can support reproducible, data-driven bio-fabrication workflows.

Methods
2′×2′×3′ controlled incubator setup · Raspberry Pi environmental monitoring · CO₂, humidity, and temperature sensors · LED-assisted time-lapse camera · Substrate mixing and inoculation · Daily data logging and growth inspection · Formwork-based colonization within CNC-routed mold

Research System
Substrate-filled formwork was placed inside a controlled incubator and observed continuously. A Raspberry Pi tracked temperature, humidity, and CO₂ every hour, while an infrared camera captured time-lapse imagery of the colonization process. The system enabled a synchronized record of environmental variables and biological behavior, contributing to a replicable growth protocol.

Findings
During the monitored growth period (April 09–14), temperature remained between 23.0–24.2 °C, humidity stabilized near 60%, and CO₂ levels peaked above 3000 ppm before gradually declining—evidence of active fungal respiration. These indicators confirmed successful colonization and validated the environmental parameters required for stable mycelium growth.
