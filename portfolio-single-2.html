CAPSTONE_make 2024

Automated Structural Logic

Everything in this project was new, chaotic, and unforgettable—from the blown capacitor to the overheated laptop to the nights spent troubleshooting with my partner. With our advisors' help, we slowly built a robotics workflow we didn't know how to begin. The result proved that rigor, more than skill, is what carries an ambitious idea to the finish line.

Abstract
CAPSTONE-make investigates how robotic automation and regenerative bio-fabrication can be coupled to produce structurally intelligent building components. The research encodes structural logic computationally before biological growth, generating composite panels whose organization emerges from both algorithmic design and mycelial behavior. The work examines how digital systems and living matter can be integrated into adaptive, materially efficient construction workflows.

Background
Fabrication workflows often separate structural design, material logic, and biological processes. CAPSTONE_make explores a hybrid approach in which robotic precision establishes a computational scaffold and biological growth completes the component. This research looks at how digitally coordinated fabrication can support regenerative, materially efficient building practices.

Methods
Robotic fabrication workflow design · Custom end-effector development · Grasshopper / Python toolpath generation · Microcontroller integration · Material and mycelium growth prototyping · Structural and geometric testing · Documentation through GSAPP Capstone

Research System
A custom end-effector mounted on a UR3 robotic arm fabricates slender wooden tension members that anchor a panel's internal scaffold. Parametric scripts generate 3D infill frameworks and translate them into tailored toolpaths. The end-effector's heated press and veneer-tape mechanism produce continuous, geometry-specific infill. These modules are then placed into formwork and grown with mycelium, linking computational logic to biological formation.

Findings
The research demonstrates how algorithmically defined structures can be completed through regenerative bio-material growth, suggesting a materially efficient workflow where biological processes and robotic automation operate as a unified fabrication system.
