CAPSTONE 2024-2025

This project reimagines acoustic panel systems through modular mycelium-based components that merge robotic precision with biological growth. It proposes a move away from extractive materials toward regenerative, sound-responsive surfaces that grow within digitally defined scaffolds.

Robotically formed wooden frameworks are fabricated through a UR3 arm equipped with a custom end-effector, then placed into modular molds and inoculated with mycelium. Growth occurs within a climate-controlled incubator that monitors COâ‚‚, temperature, and humidity. After seven days, panels undergo acoustic testing using a vacuum-tube speaker, Arduino-based recording, and comparison against cork and spray-applied systems.

The project encodes structure before growth and integrates sensing across fabrication, incubation, and evaluation. This creates a responsive workflow where biological behavior and digital logic operate in concert, resulting in panels with competitive acoustic performance and significantly lower embodied carbon.

Material prototypes refined through multiple growth cycles; environmental data collected; acoustic results validated and exhibited in the Columbia GSAPP archive.

Role
Concept development, parametric modeling, robotic fabrication workflow design, sensor integration, biological growth monitoring, acoustic evaluation
