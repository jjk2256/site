Julia Kwon is a computational designer and researcher exploring how sustainable materials and interactive systems can reshape the relationship between bodies, machines, and environments. She develops tools, objects, and spaces that integrate sensing, microcontrollers, and digital fabrication to make material behavior legible, responsive, and participatory.

She recently completed her Master of Science in Computational Design Practices at Columbia University's GSAPP, where her work focused on regenerative material systems and embodied interfaces. Her capstone project, Unpacking the Made_Mycelium Acoustic Module, proposes a materially intelligent acoustic panel that merges mycelium composites with environmental sensing and open-source fabrication. She was advised by David Benjamin.

Her background spans architecture, visual arts, and ecological computation. She received her Master of Architecture from NewSchool of Architecture and Design and her B.A. in Visual Arts from the University of Maryland, Baltimore County, where she also studied Korean art and political poster design.

Julia has contributed to large-scale infrastructure and housing projects as a designer at firms including Gensler, AECOM, and Busenhart und Partner AG. Her work has been exhibited at Columbia GSAPP, AIA Baltimore, and BeeBreeders, and recognized by awards including the Computational Design Young Voices Award.
