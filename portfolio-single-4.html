Wakefulness as Creative Balance 2024

This project began with a simple question: could a few random objects—a stepper motor, an electric guitar pickup, and a balancing board—create a new medium for attention? Each morning's bent wire and improvised recording became something to literally balance with, forming a loop between sound and presence. The innovation was in connecting those unlikely pieces into a ritual that made wakefulness feel crafted rather than consumed.

Wakefulness as Creative Balance reframes attentiveness as a state generated through two linked practices: improvised music making and balancing. Wakefulness becomes something crafted through motion, sound, and intentional engagement rather than triggered by external stimulants.

Each morning begins by hand-bending a new wire to form the day's vibrating instrument. The wire is driven by a stepper motor, its resonance captured by an electric guitar pickup and routed through a Solid State Logic audio interface into GarageBand with minimal processing. This produces a short improvised composition shaped by the wire's unique form and the conditions of the moment.

The second phase involves listening to this recording while balancing on a board. The body responds to shifting sonic textures, and those textures subtly guide posture, equilibrium, and focus. The practice forms a feedback loop between creative intention and embodied awareness—a compact ritual that cultivates wakefulness through sound and physical presence.

Materials
Hand-bent wire, stepper motor, electric guitar pickup, ESP microcontroller, arduino, Solid State Logic interface, balancing board, cables, GarageBand
