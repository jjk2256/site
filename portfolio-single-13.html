CAPSTONE_test 2024

Acoustic Performance Evaluation

This phase was a small miracle, made possible only because I refused to give up. From struggling to cut a cylindrical sample—hauling it all the way to MIT in case someone could help—to losing my advisor to an unexpected accident, the testing almost didn't happen. But with Daniel's timely guidance and a lot of stubborn persistence, the DIY setup worked and the phase closed successfully.

Abstract
The Test phase evaluates the acoustic performance of bio-fabricated mycelium modules in comparison to industry benchmarks. Using a custom-built impedance tube, the study measures sound absorption across controlled frequencies to assess the viability of mycelium composites as modular acoustic building elements.

Background
While mycelium is increasingly recognized for its ecological performance, its acoustic properties require rigorous testing to validate its architectural potential. This phase investigates how biologically grown modules compare to cork and commercial spray-applied cellulose within a controlled measurement system.

Methods
Custom acoustic testing rig (vacuum tube, speaker, receiver) · Arduino Uno + WAV Shield for signal control · Standardized cylindrical samples (mycelium, cork, SONA spray) · Frequency-controlled testing environment · Digital logging of absorption values · Cross-material comparison via consistent geometries

Research System
Each sample was placed in a sealed impedance tube where a speaker generated controlled frequencies and a receiver measured attenuation. Uniform fabrication ensured that differences in performance reflected material behavior rather than form. This process produced a calibrated dataset for comparative analysis.

Findings
Mycelium, cork, and SONA spray each scored within a 580–600 absorption range, placing mycelium squarely within competitive mid-frequency performance. Combined with its regenerative lifecycle and carbon-negative profile, the results position mycelium as a materially and environmentally credible candidate for interior acoustic applications.
