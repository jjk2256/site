Unhampering 2023

Unhampering started with a casual conversation about Shein and the shock of how fast fashion becomes global waste. It pushed us to ask a harder question: how can we deploy technology now to clean up the mess we helped manufacture in this fast economy? Designing these modular upcycling units felt like drafting a responsible future, not just a speculative one.

Unhampering reimagines the global clothing-waste crisis through a set of modular infrastructures designed to intervene in the flows of overproduction, dumping, and environmental degradation driven by fast fashion. Grounded in research on textile landfills, microplastic pollution, and transnational waste circulation, the project translates these pressures into mobile units capable of operating across diverse urban and ecological contexts.

Developed within the Speculative Cities class, the project explores how waste and urban futures intersect. Using Rhino and Grasshopper, the modules were computationally modeled for rapid assembly, disassembly, and redeployment, enabling them to adapt to shifting sites of accumulation. Each unit responds to a specific condition within the clothing-waste narrative—remediating contaminated ground, intercepting coastal waste flows, cleaning discarded textiles, and supporting global redistribution.

Core Units
01. Soil Purification Unit — Removes chemical and microplastic contaminants from degraded dumping-ground soils.
02. Seaport — Redirects unsellable or excess clothing shipments into cleaning and recycling pathways.
03. Clothing Cleaning Unit — Washes, sorts, and prepares discarded textiles for reuse or fiber-level recovery.
04. Airport — Enables rapid transport and redeployment of waste-processing units worldwide.

Role
Concept development, system design and modeling (Rhino + Grasshopper), graphic and narrative documentation
