EchoPulse 2025

EchoPulse began with a lot of questioningâ€”asking friends, asking GPT, and manually crowdsourcing ideas because I didn't want to copy any existing game mechanic. Once the system took shape, my partner and I refined it together, especially the challenge of getting the physical pulse sensors to communicate cleanly with the p5.js visualization. Its significance lies in inventing a two-player interaction built from collective thinking rather than borrowed from existing games.

EchoPulse turns the heartbeat into a shared visual performance. The project creates a biofeedback game in which players influence pulsating waveforms that reflect their physiological rhythms, revealing the subtle interplay between body and environment.

Custom pulse-sensing "play pieces" transmit biometric data to an Elegoo Mega microcontroller housed in a modular enclosure. The processed signals feed a p5.js visualization that generates concentric waves responding in real time to moment-to-moment variations in pulse.

By transforming intimate physiological signals into an expressive medium, EchoPulse extends biofeedback beyond measurement into aesthetic interaction and collective awareness.

A complete biofeedback game system; custom sensing hardware; real-time visualization refined through playtesting.

Role
Concept design, interaction design, biofeedback integration, physical computing, p5.js visualization development, prototyping
